  • What is this?

    • The Torrey Chemistry Tournament is a competition that was started by a couple of high school students that are interested in chemistry. All pre-collegiate students are allowed to take the exam. It's relatively new, so we encourage you to share it with your friends.

  • Neat - how do I get started?

    • When one of the testing windows opens, you can download the test. Before the same testing window closes, send your submission to torreychemistry@gmail.com with a proper submission title. Learn how the competition works at the competition tab.

  • Where's the test?

    • The most recent contest opened on 4/20. It will close a week from then. You can grab a copy of the test here. The IR spectrum is kind of hard to see, so you can download a clearer copy of that here.

  • What's the test format?

    • The exam consists of five free response questions comprised of multiple parts. Please write clear solutions in your responses. You must show work in order to receive credit.

    The questions are not designed to be trivial, and span a wide variety of topics. However, resources such as textbooks and Wikipedia are available for use during the exam.

    All competitors are expected to follow a code of honor. Disqualification may occur on reasonable grounds, such as, but not limited to: collaboration with other people, sharing answers, and directly copying materials from online resources without proper citation.

  • One of the questions is wrong!

    • We're human. We make errors. Send us an email with an appropriate title and we'll take a peek.

  • How do I submit my work?

    • Please send your responses to torreychemistry@gmail.com. Accepted forms include files with .pdf, .txt, and .doc/x extensions. A good idea is to practice using the markup language LaTeX.

    You must include your name, the name of your school, and your grade.
